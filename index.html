10L
Minimalist

10 Liters

The essentials-only pack

What fits inside

Phone Keys Wallet Water bottle Snack Light jacket

Pros

  • Ultra lightweight
  • Barely notice it's there
  • Great for short trips
  • Often packable / foldable

Cons

  • No laptop sleeve
  • Very limited space
  • Minimal organization
  • No support structure
Best for: Quick errands, bike rides, running to the gym
15L
Compact

15 Liters

The light commuter

What fits inside

13" laptop Charger Notebook Water bottle Lunch Sunglasses

Pros

  • Fits a small laptop
  • Sleek, low-profile look
  • Light enough to carry all day
  • Works as a personal item on flights

Cons

  • Tight fit for 15"+ laptops
  • Not much room beyond basics
  • Limited outer pockets
  • Can't pack extra layers easily
Best for: Light commutes, coffee shop work, minimalist travelers
20L
Everyday

20 Liters

The sweet spot for daily carry

What fits inside

15" laptop Charger + cables Book Water bottle Lunch Light jacket Headphones

Pros

  • Fits most laptops comfortably
  • Room for a full day's gear
  • Good pocket organization
  • Doesn't look oversized

Cons

  • Tight for overnight trips
  • Not ideal for bulky items
  • May lack hip belt support
Best for: Daily commute, school, day hikes, co-working
25L
Versatile

25 Liters

The do-everything daypack

What fits inside

16" laptop Charger + accessories Change of shirt Rain jacket Water bottle Lunch + snacks Toiletry pouch

Pros

  • Can handle a short overnight
  • Enough room without bulk
  • Multiple compartments
  • Great for day hikes with gear

Cons

  • Slightly bulky for minimal carry
  • Can get heavy when fully packed
  • Sometimes too big for personal item
Best for: Day trips, one-bag day-to-night, active commuters
30L
Weekender

30 Liters

The one-bag travel pack

What fits inside

Laptop + tablet 2-3 days of clothes Toiletries Shoes (compact) Jacket Camera Packing cubes

Pros

  • Weekend trips in one bag
  • Usually fits as carry-on
  • Great organization options
  • Often has hip belt & sternum strap

Cons

  • Overkill for daily commute
  • Noticeable weight when full
  • May need packing cubes to stay tidy
Best for: Weekend getaways, one-bag travel, photography trips
35L
Travel

35 Liters

The carry-on sweet spot

What fits inside

Laptop + chargers 4-5 days of clothes Toiletry bag Extra pair of shoes Jacket Books / gear Packing cubes

Pros

  • Max carry-on size for most airlines
  • Can do a full week if you pack light
  • Solid frame & support system
  • Often has clamshell opening

Cons

  • Too big for everyday use
  • Heavy when packed out
  • Borderline for some budget airlines
Best for: Carry-on travel, digital nomads, week-long trips
40L
Expedition

40 Liters

The max carry-on / light trekking pack

What fits inside

Full electronics kit 5-7 days of clothes Sleeping bag liner Rain gear First aid kit Cooking gear (compact) Extra shoes

Pros

  • Enough for extended travel
  • Great for multi-day hikes
  • Hip belt distributes weight well
  • Still fits most carry-on limits

Cons

  • May get gate-checked on flights
  • Heavy — needs good shoulder/hip support
  • Way too much for daily carry
  • Harder to navigate crowded places
Best for: Extended travel, backpacking trips, thru-hikes
45L
Hauler

45 Liters

The full-on adventure pack

What fits inside

Everything in 40L, plus: Sleeping bag Camp stove Extra layers Tent (if strapped) 7-10 days of gear

Pros

  • True multi-day capacity
  • Full suspension system
  • Fits sleeping bag + camp gear
  • External attachment points

Cons

  • Won't fit carry-on limits
  • Very heavy when loaded
  • Overkill for urban travel
  • Bulky to store
Best for: Multi-day treks, camping, international backpacking
