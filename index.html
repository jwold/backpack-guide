1L
Micro Sling

1 Liter

The phone-and-keys pouch

What fits inside

Phone Keys Card wallet AirPods

Pros

  • Barely there — almost weightless
  • Keeps essentials secure
  • Great for concerts & events
  • Replaces bulky pockets

Cons

  • Can't fit much more than a phone
  • No water bottle space
  • Easy to overload the strap
Best for: Concerts, festivals, dog walks, quick outings
2L
Mini Sling

2 Liters

The everyday essentials sling

What fits inside

Phone Wallet Keys Sunglasses Earbuds Small snack

Pros

  • Perfect pocket replacement
  • Comfortable all day
  • Quick-access front zip
  • Works crossbody or waist

Cons

  • No room for a water bottle
  • Can feel cramped with bulky items
  • Limited organization
Best for: Running errands, theme parks, casual outings
4L
Compact Sling

4 Liters

The sweet spot for slings

What fits inside

Phone Wallet Small water bottle Sunglasses Battery pack Snacks Compact camera

Pros

  • Fits a small water bottle
  • Enough for half-day outings
  • Multiple pockets for organizing
  • Still slim and unobtrusive

Cons

  • No tablet or laptop space
  • Gets snug with bulky items
  • Strap can dig in if overloaded
Best for: Sightseeing, farmer's markets, day trips, urban exploring
6L
Mid Sling

6 Liters

The tech-carry sling

What fits inside

iPad Mini / Kindle Phone + charger Water bottle Earbuds Notebook + pen Snacks Sunscreen

Pros

  • Fits a small tablet
  • Good for a full day out
  • Padded strap for comfort
  • Better organization than smaller slings

Cons

  • Starts to feel like a small bag
  • Can swing around when walking fast
  • Not enough for layers or jackets
Best for: Coffee shops, museums, travel days, light commutes
8L
Large Sling

8 Liters

The almost-a-backpack sling

What fits inside

iPad / small laptop Water bottle Charger + cables Light jacket Book Snacks Sunglasses

Pros

  • Fits a tablet or 11" laptop
  • Room for a light jacket
  • Easy front-swing access
  • Enough for a full day out

Cons

  • One-shoulder fatigue on long days
  • Less stable than a backpack
  • Can look bulky when packed full
Best for: Commuting, travel days, photography, day hikes
12L
Max Sling

12 Liters

The crossbody daypack

What fits inside

13" laptop Water bottle Charger + battery Jacket Lunch Book Headphones Notebook

Pros

  • Fits a 13" laptop
  • Backpack capacity, sling access
  • Multiple compartments
  • Great for commuters who hate backpacks

Cons

  • Shoulder strain when heavy
  • Basically a small backpack at this point
  • Can be awkward to swing front-to-back
  • May need stabilizer strap
Best for: Urban commute, light travel, people who prefer crossbody access

The light commuter

What fits inside

13" laptop Charger Notebook Water bottle Lunch Sunglasses

Pros

  • Fits a small laptop
  • Sleek, low-profile look
  • Light enough to carry all day
  • Works as a personal item on flights

Cons

  • Tight fit for 15"+ laptops
  • Not much room beyond basics
  • Limited outer pockets
  • Can't pack extra layers easily
Best for: Light commutes, coffee shop work, minimalist travelers
20L
Everyday

20 Liters

The sweet spot for daily carry

What fits inside

15" laptop Charger + cables Book Water bottle Lunch Light jacket Headphones

Pros

  • Fits most laptops comfortably
  • Room for a full day's gear
  • Good pocket organization
  • Doesn't look oversized

Cons

  • Tight for overnight trips
  • Not ideal for bulky items
  • May lack hip belt support
Best for: Daily commute, school, day hikes, co-working
25L
Versatile

25 Liters

The do-everything daypack

What fits inside

16" laptop Charger + accessories Change of shirt Rain jacket Water bottle Lunch + snacks Toiletry pouch

Pros

  • Can handle a short overnight
  • Enough room without bulk
  • Multiple compartments
  • Great for day hikes with gear

Cons

  • Slightly bulky for minimal carry
  • Can get heavy when fully packed
  • Sometimes too big for personal item
Best for: Day trips, one-bag day-to-night, active commuters
30L
Weekender

30 Liters

The one-bag travel pack

What fits inside

Laptop + tablet 2-3 days of clothes Toiletries Shoes (compact) Jacket Camera Packing cubes

Pros

  • Weekend trips in one bag
  • Usually fits as carry-on
  • Great organization options
  • Often has hip belt & sternum strap

Cons

  • Overkill for daily commute
  • Noticeable weight when full
  • May need packing cubes to stay tidy
Best for: Weekend getaways, one-bag travel, photography trips
35L
Travel

35 Liters

The carry-on sweet spot

What fits inside

Laptop + chargers 4-5 days of clothes Toiletry bag Extra pair of shoes Jacket Books / gear Packing cubes

Pros

  • Max carry-on size for most airlines
  • Can do a full week if you pack light
  • Solid frame & support system
  • Often has clamshell opening

Cons

  • Too big for everyday use
  • Heavy when packed out
  • Borderline for some budget airlines
Best for: Carry-on travel, digital nomads, week-long trips
40L
Expedition

40 Liters

The max carry-on / light trekking pack

What fits inside

Full electronics kit 5-7 days of clothes Sleeping bag liner Rain gear First aid kit Cooking gear (compact) Extra shoes

Pros

  • Enough for extended travel
  • Great for multi-day hikes
  • Hip belt distributes weight well
  • Still fits most carry-on limits

Cons

  • May get gate-checked on flights
  • Heavy — needs good shoulder/hip support
  • Way too much for daily carry
  • Harder to navigate crowded places
Best for: Extended travel, backpacking trips, thru-hikes
45L
Hauler

45 Liters

The full-on adventure pack

What fits inside

Everything in 40L, plus: Sleeping bag Camp stove Extra layers Tent (if strapped) 7-10 days of gear

Pros

  • True multi-day capacity
  • Full suspension system
  • Fits sleeping bag + camp gear
  • External attachment points

Cons

  • Won't fit carry-on limits
  • Very heavy when loaded
  • Overkill for urban travel
  • Bulky to store
Best for: Multi-day treks, camping, international backpacking
